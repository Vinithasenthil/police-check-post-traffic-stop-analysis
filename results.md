# Results and Observations

## Query 1: Top vehicles with the highest number of traffic stops
Result:  
The query results showed that certain vehicle numbers appeared multiple times, indicating that the same vehicles were stopped repeatedly at police check posts.

Observation:  
This helps in identifying repeat offender vehicles. Such vehicles can be monitored more closely to improve traffic enforcement and public safety.


## Query 2: Vehicles that were searched most frequently
Result:
The analysis revealed that a small group of vehicles were searched more frequently compared to others.

Observation:
Frequently searched vehicles may indicate suspicious behavior or higher risk. This insight helps law enforcement focus search efforts more effectively.



## Query 3: Arrests based on driver age group
Result: 
The results indicated that drivers in the age group of 25 to 40 had a higher number of arrests compared to other age groups.

Observation:  
This information can be useful for planning targeted awareness programs or enforcement strategies for specific age groups.



Overall Summary:
The SQL analysis provided useful insights into repeat vehicle stops, search frequency, and arrest patterns. These observations support data-driven decision-making and help improve the efficiency of police check post monitoring.
